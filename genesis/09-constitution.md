# Raiatea Constitution

**Status:** Draft  
**Version:** 0.1.0  
**Project phase:** Project Genesis — Leaving the Shore  
**Purpose:** Define the principles that protect Raiatea from economic, political, technical, or institutional capture.

---

## Preamble

Raiatea exists to help people understand complex domains, examine evidence, navigate uncertainty, make better decisions, continue learning, and contribute responsibly to shared knowledge.

Raiatea must be economically sustainable, technically useful, open to criticism, and structurally resistant to capture.

No founder, company, investor, government, donor, customer, community majority, technical maintainer, or model provider should be able to purchase or silently acquire authority over what counts as evidence or knowledge in Raiatea.

---

## Article 1 — The person remains the captain

Raiatea may recommend routes, explanations, sources, and actions.

It must not silently replace human judgment.

Users must be able to inspect why a recommendation was produced, which evidence supports it, which assumptions were made, and what alternatives exist.

---

## Article 2 — Evidence, inference, and interpretation remain distinct

Raiatea must preserve the distinction between:

- source material;
- observation;
- extracted claim;
- inference;
- causal hypothesis;
- interpretation;
- prediction;
- recommendation.

The system must never present an inference as if it were a directly observed fact.

---

## Article 3 — No knowledge without provenance

Every meaningful public claim must remain traceable, where possible, to:

- source;
- author or origin;
- time;
- acquisition method;
- transformation history;
- model or parser version;
- reviewer or contributor;
- confidence and limitations.

---

## Article 4 — Public history cannot be silently rewritten

Public knowledge bundles, governance decisions, model revisions, and transparency-log checkpoints must be versioned and auditable.

Corrections are allowed and encouraged. Silent erasure is not.

Superseded claims remain historically inspectable unless law, safety, or privacy requires restricted access.

---

## Article 5 — Private knowledge belongs to the user

Users control their private corpus, notes, learning history, reading activity, interests, and private models.

Raiatea must support:

- local-first use where feasible;
- export;
- deletion;
- provider migration;
- encryption;
- explicit sharing choices.

Private corpus content does not become community knowledge without deliberate consent and lawful processing.

---

## Article 6 — Money cannot purchase epistemic privilege

No payment may purchase:

- favourable factual ranking;
- removal of documented criticism;
- suppression of public evidence;
- undisclosed sponsorship;
- a different public factual record;
- control over canonical conclusions;
- influence proportional only to capital, advertising spend, donations, or hardware.

Commercial relationships relevant to acquisition, ranking, research, or governance must be disclosed.

---

## Article 7 — Raiatea must remain independently implementable

The shared protocol, public schemas, verification formats, and export formats must remain implementable by independent parties.

No single company should be indispensable for:

- reading public knowledge;
- verifying provenance;
- operating a node;
- exporting user data;
- participating in federation;
- preserving the public history of the system.

---

## Article 8 — Governance cannot determine truth by majority vote

Voting and governance may decide:

- priorities;
- budgets;
- investigations;
- moderation policy;
- protocol changes;
- resource allocation.

Voting must not determine whether a claim is true.

Epistemic status must depend on evidence, source quality, method, reproducibility, and transparent reasoning.

---

## Article 9 — Disagreement must be preserved

Raiatea must support:

- competing interpretations;
- minority reports;
- unresolved status;
- domain-specific models;
- principled forks;
- appeals and revisions.

Consensus is useful when supported by evidence. Forced convergence is prohibited.

---

## Article 10 — No hidden ranking logic for consequential outputs

For consequential searches, profiles, briefings, public-reality dossiers, and recommendations, users must be able to understand the main factors that influenced inclusion, ranking, omission, confidence, and relevance.

The full implementation may remain complex, but the operative reasons must be inspectable.

---

## Article 11 — Attention is not a commodity

Raiatea must not optimize primarily for engagement, addiction, scrolling time, or notification volume.

It should optimize for:

- understanding;
- uncertainty reduction;
- time saved;
- relevance;
- user autonomy;
- lasting competence.

The system should be allowed to say that nothing important changed.

---

## Article 12 — Useful computation only

Community-contributed computation must perform inspectable and reusable work.

Raiatea must not require artificial proof-of-work or wasteful computation merely to create scarcity or influence.

Energy use, verification quality, and environmental cost should be measured.

---

## Article 13 — Public-interest analysis must not become surveillance

Profiles of public actors must be limited to legitimate public-interest purposes.

Raiatea must apply necessity, proportionality, context, correction rights, and safety review.

It must not facilitate harassment, targeting of families, exposure of private addresses, or aggregation of irrelevant sensitive information.

---

## Article 14 — The commons cannot be sold

A commercial company may sell services around Raiatea.

It must not own or sell exclusive control over:

- the protocol constitution;
- public knowledge commons;
- public transparency history;
- user portability;
- independent node operation;
- the right to verify public artefacts.

A commercial company may fail or be sold without selling Raiatea itself.

---

## Article 15 — Concentration must be measured

Raiatea must monitor concentration in:

- revenue;
- donors;
- compute;
- storage;
- search and indexing;
- identity;
- governance;
- hosted usage;
- source access;
- model providers.

Formal decentralisation is insufficient when practical control remains concentrated.

---

## Article 16 — High-impact changes require constitutional review

Changes affecting mission, user ownership, public auditability, protocol openness, revenue firewalls, governance balance, or epistemic transparency require:

- public proposal;
- published rationale;
- review period;
- conflict-of-interest disclosure;
- supermajority across multiple constituencies;
- delayed activation;
- preserved right to compatible fork.

---

## Article 17 — Raiatea must prove its value

Raiatea must not rely only on philosophical ambition.

Each product or major capability must define measurable user outcomes, such as:

- time saved;
- quality of understanding;
- reduction in factual errors;
- citation completeness;
- learning retention;
- better-calibrated forecasts;
- improved decision confidence without increased dependency;
- ability to explain conclusions independently.

A capability that cannot demonstrate value should be revised, narrowed, or removed.

---

## Article 18 — The constitution protects principles, not current implementations

No current model, database, provider, architecture, company, or governance mechanism is sacred.

Implementations may change when better approaches are found.

The protected object is the mission and the user's ability to verify, leave, fork, and think independently.

---

## Closing principle

> Raiatea should be profitable enough to survive, useful enough to deserve continued use, and structurally unable to become an authority that demands trust without verification.
