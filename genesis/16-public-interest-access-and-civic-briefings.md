# Public-Interest Access and Civic Briefings

**Status:** Draft  
**Version:** 0.1.0  
**Project phase:** Project Genesis — Leaving the Shore  
**Purpose:** Define which continuous updates should remain freely accessible because they concern public-interest, civic, environmental, democratic, scientific, or safety-relevant matters.

---

## 1. Core principle

Raiatea may charge for advanced monitoring, personalisation, private-source integration, expert review, and high-frequency professional services.

However, a minimum layer of public-interest knowledge should remain freely accessible when it is important for civic participation, democratic accountability, public safety, environmental awareness, or access to essential scientific understanding.

> No person should be excluded from basic civic orientation because they cannot afford a subscription.

---

## 2. Public-interest domains

The free public-interest layer may include topics such as:

- elections and public institutions;
- laws, regulations, and public policies;
- environmental risks and climate-related developments;
- public health and safety;
- civil rights and liberties;
- war, peace, and international crises;
- public spending and major public contracts;
- scientific corrections with broad social impact;
- emergency information;
- major changes affecting education, work, welfare, or essential services;
- public statements and actions of office-holders;
- misinformation campaigns with substantial public consequences.

The list should remain governed by public-interest criteria rather than party, ideology, or commercial value.

---

## 3. Minimum free update layer

For a monitored public-interest route, the free layer should provide at least:

- major verified developments;
- concise explanation of what changed;
- source links and provenance;
- distinction between established facts and disputed interpretations;
- corrections to previous public information;
- critical changes in laws, policies, risks, or institutional decisions;
- major changes in a public actor's declared position or documented actions;
- access to the public timeline and bibliography;
- a low-frequency digest such as weekly or event-driven updates.

This free layer does not need to include unlimited private personalisation, high-frequency alerts, premium connectors, or expert-reviewed custom reports.

---

## 4. Paid civic and professional layers

Paid plans may add:

- daily or real-time monitoring;
- personalised relevance to the user's location, work, interests, or previous routes;
- custom source lists;
- private document integration;
- advanced comparison between political programmes, decisions, and outcomes;
- historical scenario analysis;
- team collaboration;
- exportable professional dossiers;
- expert review;
- API access;
- long-term archival and audit;
- alerts based on custom conditions.

The paid service enhances depth, speed, and personal relevance. It must not hide essential public corrections or create different public facts for paying users.

---

## 5. Public-interest test

A development may qualify for the free layer when several of the following are true:

- it affects a large number of people;
- it concerns fundamental rights or democratic participation;
- delay may cause material harm;
- the information is necessary to evaluate public power;
- the issue has significant environmental or intergenerational consequences;
- misinformation is widespread and consequential;
- the underlying evidence is publicly verifiable;
- the update corrects a material error previously circulated;
- access supports informed civic decision-making.

No single political authority, donor, advertiser, or company should unilaterally decide which topics receive this status.

---

## 6. Funding the free layer

Possible funding sources include:

- a fixed share of commercial revenue;
- Foundation funds;
- memberships and donations;
- public-interest grants;
- library, university, or newsroom partnerships;
- institutional sponsorship with strict editorial firewalls;
- cross-subsidy from professional monitoring plans;
- community compute and volunteer review;
- endowment income.

Funding relationships must be disclosed and must not influence factual conclusions, topic suppression, or ranking.

---

## 7. No two-tier public reality

Raiatea must not create a system in which:

- paying citizens receive important corrections while others remain misinformed;
- public officials' actions are visible only behind a paywall;
- environmental or safety warnings are delayed for free users;
- political evidence is ranked differently by subscription level;
- sponsors can reserve public-interest analysis for selected audiences.

A paid user may receive faster, deeper, or more personalised interpretation, but the underlying public evidence and major verified updates should remain common.

---

## 8. Local and multilingual access

Public-interest information should be available in accessible language and, where feasible:

- local languages;
- simplified summaries;
- source-linked detailed views;
- printable and shareable formats;
- low-bandwidth delivery;
- accessible design for disabilities;
- local geographic context;
- educational explanations of institutions and terminology.

Public usefulness requires more than nominal free access.

---

## 9. Anti-propaganda safeguards

The free civic layer is especially vulnerable to political pressure and coordinated manipulation.

It should therefore require:

- primary-source preference;
- source-family analysis;
- public methodology;
- visible uncertainty;
- competing sourced interpretations;
- correction history;
- independent witnesses;
- conflict-of-interest disclosure;
- appeals and minority reports;
- no political advertising inside factual dossiers.

---

## 10. Relationship with completed expeditions

A user who completes an expedition on a public-interest topic may continue to receive:

### Free minimum watch

- major changes;
- public corrections;
- weekly or event-driven briefing;
- public sources and updated timeline.

### Paid enhanced watch

- daily monitoring;
- private comparison with the user's prior model;
- custom actors, regions, policies, and source lists;
- deeper historical and causal analysis;
- professional exports and alerts.

The distinction should be visible before the user subscribes.

---

## 11. Metrics

Raiatea should measure:

- public-interest updates delivered freely;
- time between verification and public release;
- corrections reaching free and paid users equally;
- geographic and language coverage;
- missed major civic events;
- political or donor concentration;
- user understanding of facts versus interpretations;
- accessibility and low-bandwidth usage;
- percentage of civic routes with a maintained free watch.

---

## 12. Constitutional extension

The following principle should be considered for future inclusion in the Constitution:

> Essential public-interest evidence and major verified updates must not be withheld solely because a person cannot pay.

This principle does not require every analysis, private service, or high-cost workflow to be free. It protects a minimum common informational floor necessary for civic life.

---

## 13. Provisional decision

Raiatea will provide a free minimum update layer for monitored topics of significant civic, democratic, environmental, scientific, safety, or public-interest importance.

Paid services may add speed, depth, private personalisation, professional tools, and expert review, but must not create separate public realities.

> Raiatea may sell a better telescope. It must not put the shared horizon behind a paywall.
