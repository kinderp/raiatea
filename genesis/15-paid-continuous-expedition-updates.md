# Paid Continuous Expedition Updates

**Status:** Draft  
**Version:** 0.1.0  
**Project phase:** Project Genesis — Leaving the Shore  
**Purpose:** Define how Raiatea may monetize continuous monitoring and updates for completed expeditions while preserving user trust, attention, and independence.

---

## 1. Core idea

A completed expedition should remain connected to the evolving field.

Raiatea may offer paid continuous monitoring that watches relevant sources, detects meaningful changes, updates the user's route and models, and produces concise daily, weekly, or event-driven briefings.

The paid value is not access to public facts themselves. It is the ongoing work required to:

- monitor many sources;
- filter noise;
- detect changes;
- compare them with the user's existing understanding;
- explain why they matter;
- revise the expedition;
- preserve history;
- save the user's attention.

> Users pay for continuity, relevance, verification, and time saved—not for ownership of knowledge.

---

## 2. What the service updates

A monitored expedition may receive updates to:

- timeline;
- bibliography;
- people and organisations to follow;
- current positions and declared changes;
- source quality;
- prerequisite map;
- research state of the art;
- open questions;
- process models;
- forecasts;
- exercises and projects;
- recommendations;
- generated learning modules;
- obsolete or superseded explanations.

Each update should identify exactly which previous element changed.

---

## 3. Update frequencies

Possible plans:

### Essential watch

- only major changes;
- low-frequency checks;
- monthly or event-driven digest;
- community compute when available.

### Personal continuous briefing

- daily or weekly digest;
- monitored people, papers, repositories, blogs, news, and public sources;
- personalised relevance scoring;
- route revision suggestions;
- source-linked explanations.

### Professional watch

For researchers, newsrooms, companies, and institutions:

- high-frequency monitoring;
- custom source lists;
- alerts for retractions, releases, legal changes, position changes, or emerging controversies;
- team collaboration;
- auditable briefings;
- API and export;
- service guarantees.

### Deep review

- periodic expert-reviewed state-of-the-field report;
- recalculation of process models;
- bibliography audit;
- obsolete-source analysis;
- forecast resolution and recalibration;
- recommended route redesign.

## 4. Free and paid boundary

The basic public knowledge and community-created updates should remain accessible under the applicable commons rules.

Paid services may cover:

- private personalisation;
- continuous source monitoring;
- faster processing;
- larger watch lists;
- premium connectors;
- private-source integration;
- expert review;
- guaranteed delivery;
- historical comparison;
- custom alert rules;
- organisation-level collaboration;
- long-term archival and audit.

Raiatea should not create artificial delays or hide important public corrections solely to force payment.

A user who stops paying should retain:

- the expedition snapshot already created;
- exported artefacts;
- public updates available to everyone;
- their private corpus and notes;
- portability to another provider.

They may lose the managed monitoring service, not their accumulated knowledge.

---

## 5. Why this can be a strong recurring business

The initial expedition solves an immediate problem. The monitoring service solves a recurring one:

> I understood this field once, but I do not have time to continuously reconstruct what changed.

This is especially valuable for:

- AI engineers and researchers;
- journalists;
- teachers;
- professionals in regulated domains;
- investors and analysts;
- public administrations;
- citizens following complex public issues;
- students preparing long courses or examinations.

The service becomes more valuable as the route becomes richer, because Raiatea can compare each new event against the user's exact prior model.

---

## 6. Briefing quality

A paid briefing must not become a conventional news feed.

Every item should answer:

- What changed?
- Which source reports it?
- Is the source primary or derivative?
- Why does it matter for this expedition?
- Which previous model, claim, source, or recommendation is affected?
- Is it a confirmation, contradiction, correction, extension, or unresolved signal?
- What action, if any, is recommended?
- How much time is required to inspect it?

A briefing should also say when no meaningful change occurred.

---

## 7. Anti-engagement safeguards

Recurring revenue creates pressure to increase notifications and make the service appear constantly active.

Raiatea must resist this incentive.

Required safeguards:

1. Billing must not depend on number of notifications.
2. The system may send an empty briefing when nothing important changed.
3. Users control frequency, depth, and interruption level.
4. Every included item has an inspectable relevance reason.
5. Every omitted category can be reviewed.
6. The service measures attention saved, not time spent in the app.
7. Cancellation must be simple.
8. The knowledge snapshot remains exportable after cancellation.

> A trustworthy subscription sometimes proves its value by refusing to manufacture urgency.

---

## 8. Shared benefit from paid monitoring

A paid watch may discover public sources, corrections, retractions, or new process relationships.

After privacy, rights, and quality review, reusable derived knowledge should be eligible to return to the shared commons.

The user pays for:

- private monitoring;
- personal comparison;
- speed;
- compute;
- customisation;
- expert review.

The community may still benefit from:

- public source references;
- new claims;
- corrections;
- public timelines;
- process model revisions;
- verification results;
- general learning modules.

Thus paid use can finance and improve public knowledge without exposing the user's private interests or corpus.

---

## 9. Pricing principles

Possible pricing dimensions:

- number of active expeditions;
- update frequency;
- number and cost of monitored sources;
- private corpus size;
- compute intensity;
- expert review;
- team size;
- retention and archival period;
- service guarantees;
- specialised connectors.

Pricing should not depend on political sensitivity, user vulnerability, or how important an update is.

There should be accessible plans for students, educators, independent researchers, and citizens, possibly subsidised by institutions or the Foundation.

---

## 10. Metrics

The service should measure:

- percentage of briefing items judged useful;
- time saved compared with manual monitoring;
- important developments missed;
- false alarms;
- source diversity;
- corrections delivered before user action;
- updates that materially changed a route;
- notification burden;
- retention based on declared usefulness rather than habit;
- successful export and cancellation.

## 12. Provisional decision

Raiatea may charge recurring fees for continuously monitoring and updating an expedition.

The subscription pays for monitoring, filtering, personalisation, compute, and review. It must not restrict access to public truth, manipulate attention, or make accumulated knowledge dependent on continued payment.

> The expedition may end; the field does not. Raiatea can be paid to keep watch, but the user must remain free to leave with the map already built.
