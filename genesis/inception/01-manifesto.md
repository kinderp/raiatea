## 1. Purpose and limits

This Manifesto states the commitments that should remain visible while Raiatea
changes its products, models, storage and interfaces. It is shorter and less
prescriptive than a constitution. It does not decide a legal structure,
governance mechanism, ontology or technical architecture.

Acceptance of this document would accept only the statements labelled
`principle` or `accepted-decision`. Candidate commitments remain provisional
until they receive explicit maintainer review.

## 2. Founding declaration

> Assertion status: `accepted-decision`

Raiatea exists to help a person turn fragmented sources and repeated model
interactions into a durable route toward understanding: a route that can be
inspected, corrected, continued, exported and connected to later work.

Raiatea does not seek to become an authority that knows on behalf of the
person. It seeks to make sources, transformations, uncertainty, alternatives
and consequences easier to navigate.

## 3. Accepted commitments

The following principles were accepted through the canonical
[`Why Raiatea`](00-why-raiatea.md). They apply before the complete platform
exists.

### 3.1 The person remains the navigator

> Assertion status: `principle`

Raiatea may recommend routes, surface contradictions and explain trade-offs.
The person retains authority over goals, private state, publication and
decisions. Important recommendations must remain inspectable and contestable.

### 3.2 No important conclusion without a route back

> Assertion status: `principle`

A meaningful synthesis must remain connected to its sources, transformations,
uncertainty and time. Citation is not decoration added after generation;
provenance is part of the result.

### 3.3 Understanding must be demonstrated through use

> Assertion status: `principle`

Raiatea should prefer explanation, reconstruction, application, correction
and recovery over the appearance of fluent completion. Reading time, clicks
and isolated answers are observed interactions, not automatic proof of
competence.

### 3.4 Private material is not automatically publishable material

> Assertion status: `principle`

The ability to process a source for private study does not grant the right to
redistribute it. Private corpora, personal notes, learner evidence, derived
knowledge and public artifacts require explicit boundaries, rights and
consent.

### 3.5 Architecture must follow validated value

> Assertion status: `principle`

Graphs, agents, federation, distributed execution and governance are means.
They are justified when they improve a validated user outcome or protect a
required property. Raiatea must not hide the absence of value behind the size
of its architecture.

## 4. Candidate enduring commitments

The following statements derive from the exploratory Genesis notes. They are
strong candidates for the future Manifesto but remain provisional in this
draft.

### 4.1 Preserve evidence before interpretation

> Assertion status: `provisional-decision`

Source material, extracted content, normalization, translation, adaptation,
derived values, inference and hypothesis should remain distinguishable. A
later explanation must not silently replace the material from which it was
constructed.

When the available evidence is insufficient, Raiatea should expose the gap
rather than manufacture a complete route.

### 4.2 Preserve change instead of overwriting history

> Assertion status: `provisional-decision`

Claims, sources, explanations, learner routes and forecasts can change. A
correction should add a visible revision, not erase the state needed to
understand how the present conclusion emerged.

Integrity, authenticity, accuracy and interpretation answer different
questions and must not be collapsed into one trust label.

### 4.3 Add pedagogical value or remain close to the source

> Assertion status: `provisional-decision`

Translation, animation and generated exercises are justified only when they
reduce avoidable effort or improve accurate explanation, application,
transfer or recall for the intended learner. More content and more motion are
not evidence of better teaching.

The original source should remain reachable where rights and availability
permit. Raiatea's explanation must declare its simplifications and limits.

### 4.4 Prefer open, portable and replaceable foundations

> Assertion status: `provisional-decision`

Users should be able to export durable artifacts and continue their work when
a model, provider or application changes. Shared schemas, adapters, benchmark
harnesses and lawful fixtures should be open where doing so does not expose
private data, credentials or protected corpora.

Provider independence does not require rebuilding mature engines. Raiatea
should reuse and compare existing tools behind replaceable contracts whenever
evidence supports that choice.

### 4.5 Keep disagreement and uncertainty visible

> Assertion status: `provisional-decision`

Raiatea should not convert repetition into corroboration or disagreement into
a misleading average. Competing explanations, counter-evidence, missing
observations and unresolved contradictions should remain inspectable.

The system may help evaluate evidence; it must not ask a vote, popularity
metric or single global reputation score to determine truth.

### 4.6 Protect attention and autonomy

> Assertion status: `provisional-decision`

Raiatea should optimize for useful orientation, understanding, time saved and
deliberate return. It should be allowed to report that nothing important
changed. Engagement, notification volume and time on screen are not success
metrics by themselves.

### 4.7 Resist capture without freezing implementation

> Assertion status: `provisional-decision`

Money, institutional status, compute, popularity or ownership of a hosted
service must not purchase hidden epistemic privilege. At the same time, no
current database, model, company form or interface should be treated as
sacred. Durable principles should constrain replaceable implementations.

The legal and governance structures needed to enforce this commitment remain
future work and require specialist review.

## 5. How Raiatea should work

> Assertion status: `provisional-decision`

Every useful Raiatea workflow should move through a visible subset of this
cycle:

```text
Acquire or reference a lawful source
  -> preserve structure, context and provenance
  -> distinguish observations, claims and transformations
  -> construct an explanation or route
  -> expose alternatives, uncertainty and limits
  -> invite use, verification and correction
  -> preserve the resulting evidence and revision
```

The cycle is open. A durable artifact is a maintained model of the route, not
a claim that inquiry is finished.

## 6. What this Manifesto does not promise

> Assertion status: `accepted-decision`

This Manifesto does not promise that Raiatea will:

- ingest every source perfectly;
- infer truth or causality automatically;
- replace authors, teachers, researchers or professional judgment;
- publish private or protected material merely because it can process it;
- produce one canonical answer for every dispute;
- support high-stakes automated decisions without domain-specific controls;
- build a universal ontology before validating concrete work;
- compete with frontier model providers on raw model capability.

## 7. Accountability

> Assertion status: `accepted-decision`

Raiatea must test its claims against strong alternatives, including the
original source and ordinary use of a capable general-purpose model. A feature
that cannot create a more accurate, durable, reproducible, updateable,
portable or governable outcome should be narrowed, removed or left to the
simpler tool.

Failure of the largest vision does not invalidate every reusable artifact.
Open adapters, provenance contracts, pedagogical modules and evaluation
methods may retain value independently.
