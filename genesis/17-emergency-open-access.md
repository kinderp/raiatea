# Emergency Open Access

**Status:** Draft  
**Version:** 0.1.0  
**Project phase:** Project Genesis — Leaving the Shore  
**Purpose:** Define when Raiatea must temporarily provide its highest available public-interest service level free of charge during exceptional events.

---

## 1. Core principle

During exceptional events that materially affect life, safety, democratic participation, civil rights, or collective decision-making, Raiatea should suspend ordinary paywall distinctions for the affected public-interest routes and provide the best available verified service freely to everyone.

Examples include:

- wars and major armed conflicts;
- elections, referendums, and constitutional crises;
- natural disasters;
- pandemics and major public-health emergencies;
- large environmental disasters;
- severe infrastructure failures;
- major civil emergencies;
- mass-disinformation campaigns with immediate public consequences.

> In a shared emergency, the best available orientation must become a common good.

---

## 2. What “best available level” means

Emergency open access may include, where technically and legally possible:

- high-frequency monitoring;
- real-time or near-real-time alerts;
- full public timelines;
- source-linked evidence dossiers;
- multilingual summaries;
- local and geographic relevance;
- public-actor statements and actions;
- misinformation and source-origin analysis;
- correction alerts;
- laws, measures, restrictions, and institutional decisions;
- expert-reviewed explanations;
- accessible and low-bandwidth formats;
- printable emergency briefs;
- APIs or feeds for newsrooms, schools, civil-protection bodies, libraries, and public-interest organisations;
- archived snapshots and transparent change history.

Private corpora, confidential organisational data, and personalised commercial services remain protected. Emergency access concerns public-interest knowledge and analysis.

---

## 3. No artificial downgrade for non-paying users

During an activated emergency period, Raiatea must not reserve materially important public information for paying users.

It must not create differences such as:

- faster correction alerts only for subscribers;
- better factual dossiers only for paying citizens;
- safety warnings delayed behind a paywall;
- election-integrity evidence available only to professional plans;
- higher-quality public summaries only for commercial customers.

Paid services may still cover unrelated private work, custom internal analysis, dedicated support, or institution-specific infrastructure, but not superior access to essential public facts and public-interest interpretation.

---

## 4. Activation criteria

Emergency open access should be activated through transparent criteria rather than discretion by a single executive, donor, government, or commercial partner.

Possible signals include:

- official emergency declarations;
- significant threat to life or public safety;
- national or regional elections and referendums;
- armed conflict affecting civilian populations;
- severe disruption of essential services;
- urgent need for widely accessible scientific or health information;
- evidence of coordinated manipulation capable of materially affecting public decisions;
- request from a qualified multi-stakeholder emergency body.

Activation may be global, national, regional, local, or topic-specific.

---

## 5. Governance of activation

A possible emergency mechanism should include:

1. A public activation notice.
2. The event, territory, and topics covered.
3. The criteria used.
4. The services made free.
5. The expected review date.
6. Disclosed conflicts of interest.
7. Independent oversight.
8. Automatic expiration unless renewed publicly.

Urgent provisional activation may be allowed for a short period, but continued activation should require multi-stakeholder review.

Emergency powers must not become permanent or be used to centralise governance.

---

## 6. Election mode

During elections and referendums, emergency open access may provide:

- candidate and party profiles based on public records;
- chronological statements, votes, decisions, and documented actions;
- programme comparison;
- source-linked fact checks;
- conflict-of-interest disclosures;
- electoral-law changes;
- public spending and advertising transparency;
- misinformation tracking;
- polling-method explanations;
- voting procedures and deadlines;
- visible separation between fact, interpretation, forecast, and opinion.

Raiatea must not recommend whom to vote for.

It should help citizens inspect the evidence and make their own decision.

---

## 7. War and conflict mode

During armed conflict, Raiatea may provide:

- verified event timelines;
- treaties, resolutions, and diplomatic documents;
- claims from involved parties with source provenance;
- independently verified civilian-impact information;
- documented changes in public positions;
- historical context and competing causal models;
- maps with uncertainty and timestamping;
- propaganda and source-network analysis;
- correction history;
- humanitarian and safety information from authoritative organisations.

The system must avoid exposing sensitive operational details that could endanger people.

---

## 8. Disaster and pandemic mode

During natural disasters or pandemics, Raiatea may prioritise:

- official alerts;
- evacuation or safety guidance;
- verified health recommendations;
- changes in scientific understanding;
- local service availability;
- transport and infrastructure disruption;
- emergency contacts;
- false or dangerous claims;
- translation into relevant local languages;
- low-bandwidth and offline distribution.

High-stakes guidance must be clearly sourced, dated, jurisdiction-aware, and updated when authorities or scientific evidence change.

---

## 9. Funding the emergency layer

Emergency access may be funded through:

- a permanent emergency reserve;
- a fixed share of subscription and enterprise revenue;
- Foundation funds;
- donations and rapid-response grants;
- public-interest partnerships;
- institutional sponsorship protected by editorial firewalls;
- volunteer and distributed compute;
- temporary compute donations from organisations;
- endowment income.

The emergency budget should be audited after each activation.

No sponsor may influence the factual output or suppress relevant evidence.

---

## 10. Capacity and graceful degradation

Exceptional events may create extreme demand.

Raiatea should prepare for:

- mirrored public briefings;
- static downloadable snapshots;
- distributed caches;
- peer-to-peer replication;
- low-bandwidth feeds;
- priority queues for safety-critical processing;
- suspension of nonessential compute;
- community-contributed hardware;
- open data exports for independent redistribution.

If capacity is insufficient, degradation should affect cosmetic or personalised functions before essential public information.

---

## 11. Anti-abuse safeguards

Emergency mode may be attacked by states, campaigns, coordinated communities, or opportunistic actors.

Required safeguards include:

- independent source verification;
- multiple transparency witnesses;
- strict provenance;
- public correction logs;
- source-family analysis;
- rate limiting and anti-Sybil measures;
- separation of evidence from discussion;
- preservation of minority reports;
- clear moderation rules;
- no political advertising;
- no emergency collection of unnecessary personal data.

---

## 12. Exit and post-event review

Emergency access should end only after a public review of:

- whether the exceptional condition has ended;
- which services remain of lasting public value;
- what errors occurred;
- what information was missed;
- whether any actor influenced coverage;
- what costs were incurred;
- what knowledge should remain freely available permanently;
- what changes are required before the next emergency.

All public emergency artefacts, correction histories, and governance decisions should remain archived and verifiable.

---

## 13. Constitutional extension

The following principle should be considered a protected constitutional article:

> During exceptional events affecting life, safety, democratic participation, or collective freedom, Raiatea must provide the highest feasible level of verified public-interest information freely and without commercial discrimination.

---

## 14. Provisional decision

Raiatea will support a transparent, temporary Emergency Open Access mode.

When activated, the highest feasible public-interest monitoring, verification, explanation, and briefing capabilities become free for the affected topics and populations.

> In ordinary times, premium services may finance the commons. In exceptional times, the commons takes precedence.
